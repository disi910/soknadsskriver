# Analysere stillingsannonsen

## Purpose

This file teaches you to systematically extract everything you need from a Norwegian job ad before writing. A good søknad is impossible without this step.

## Extraction Checklist

Go through the ad and extract each of these into a mental model:

### 1. Basic Info
- Company name (use their exact casing/spelling)
- Role title (use the exact title from the ad)
- Contact person name (if listed, this is gold for the opening)
- Application deadline
- Location

### 2. Qualification Tiers

Norwegian ads typically separate qualifications into two groups. Identify which is which:

**Nødvendige kvalifikasjoner (must-haves):**
Often introduced with "Vi søker deg som har...", "Krav til stillingen:", "Du må ha..."
These are non-negotiable. The søknad MUST address every single one with concrete evidence.

**Ønskede kvalifikasjoner (nice-to-haves):**
Often introduced with "Det er en fordel om du...", "Vi setter pris på...", "Ønskelig med..."
Address as many as you can. Do not apologize for gaps, simply skip what you cannot address. eStudie.no advises that it is worth applying if you meet roughly one-third or more of the desired qualifications.

If the ad does not explicitly separate them, infer: education requirements and years of experience are typically must-haves; specific tools, certifications, and personality traits are typically nice-to-haves.

### 3. Keywords

Mark every noun and adjective describing what they want. Common Norwegian job ad keywords:

**Skills/traits:** samarbeidsevner, analytisk, selvstendig, strukturert, løsningsorientert, nysgjerrig, engasjert, initiativrik, kommunikasjonsevner
**Work style:** selvstendig, i team, tverrfaglig, agilt, proaktiv
**Technical:** programmering, dataanalyse, maskinlæring, systemutvikling, skytjenester

Use these EXACT words back in the søknad. If they say "samarbeidsevner," write "samarbeidsevner", not "evne til å jobbe med andre" or a synonym. This matters for both human readers and ATS systems.

### 4. Tone Analysis

The ad's own tone tells you how to write:

| Ad signal | Your tone |
|-----------|-----------|
| Uses "du" and casual language | Warm, direct, open with "Hei" or "Hei [Fornavn]" |
| Corporate/formal language | Professional but still warm, avoid stiffness |
| English job ad from Norwegian company | Write in English unless told otherwise |
| Nynorsk ad | Write in nynorsk |
| Humor or personality in the ad | Match it, show some personality |
| Government/public sector format | More structured, address criteria explicitly |

### 5. Company Research Signals

Note anything the ad reveals about the company you can reference:
- Growth stage ("Vi er i sterk vekst", "nyoppstartet")
- Values or mission statements
- Specific products, clients, or projects mentioned
- Team size or structure
- Recent news, awards, or milestones

If the ad is thin on company info, use `web_search` to find 1-2 specific things to reference. This is one of the strongest differentiators in Norwegian applications.

### 6. Specific Content Requirements

Some ads dictate what the søknad must contain, e.g.:
- "Søknaden skal inneholde..."
- "Motivasjonsbrevet må svare på følgende spørsmål..."
- "Vi ønsker at du beskriver..."

If these exist, they override the default paragraph structure. Build the søknad around their requirements. This is common and should be treated as the primary structural guide when present.

## Output

After analysis, you should have:
1. A ranked list of qualifications to address (must-haves first)
2. A keyword list to mirror
3. A tone calibration
4. 1-2 company-specific details for the motivation paragraph
5. Identified gaps between the user's CV and the ad (these drive follow-up questions)
