## 1. Structure and Length

**Exactly 300-350 words across 3-4 paragraphs.** Never exceed one A4 page.

**Exception:** If the ad specifies what the motivasjonsbrev must contain (e.g. "søknaden skal inneholde..."), structure the søknad around those requirements instead of the default template. This may push word count to 350-400, which is fine as long as it stays on one page. The ad's requirements always take priority over the default structure.

The søknad must have a **"rød tråd"** (red thread), a coherent narrative that connects past experience to the applied role. Norwegian recruiters explicitly look for this: a logical line from who you are, through what you have done, to why this specific role is the natural next step. Every paragraph should advance this narrative.

```
Paragraph 1: Innledning (2-4 sentences)
  → Hook + who you are + why this role

Paragraph 2: Hoveddel (5-8 sentences, or split into two shorter paragraphs)
  → Your qualifications matched to their requirements
  → Concrete examples using STAR method (Situasjon, Oppgave, Handling, Resultat)

Paragraph 3: Avslutning (2-3 sentences)
  → Why you + this company = good match
  → Forward-looking closing
  → "Med vennlig hilsen" + full name
```

For internship/entry-level candidates: education comes before work experience. Lead with what you are studying and relevant projects, then supplement with part-time work or extracurriculars.

## 2. Tone and Language

### Register: Warm professional
The target tone is a confident student talking to a respected professional they want to impress, direct, specific, warm, occasionally showing personality, but always substantive. Think "engasjert og jordnær", not "stiv og formell."

**Finding your voice:** NTNU Karriere recommends asking yourself verbally: "Hvorfor vil jeg jobbe hos denne bedriften?" Then write down your answer. This produces authentic, conversational text that avoids both bureaucratic stiffness and AI-generated blandness.

**The blush test (Janteloven calibration):** Aftenposten's career desk: "Du har truffet riktig når du rødmer litt når du leser søknaden din i speilet, men samtidig ikke sier noe du ikke kan stå for." If reading your own søknad makes you blush slightly but you can defend every word, you have the right balance.

### Language level: B2-C1 Norwegian
Use everyday vocabulary (hverdagsspråk). Prefer short, common words over academic alternatives:
- "bruke" over "benytte"
- "vise" over "demonstrere"  
- "viktig" over "essensiell"
- "hjelpe" over "bistå"
- "jobbe" over "arbeide" (context dependent, "arbeide" is fine in formal contexts)
- "mål" over "målsetting"

### Pronouns and address
- Use "du/dere", never "De" (archaic and overly formal)
- Vary sentence subjects to avoid excessive "jeg-form." Tekna's rule: do not start more than two consecutive sentences with "Jeg"
- Good alternatives to starting with "Jeg": "Gjennom studiene mine...", "I rollen som...", "Som del av...", "Det som tiltrekker meg...", "Prosjektet ga meg..."

### Greetings
- If contact person is named: "Hei [Fornavn]," or jump straight into content
- If no contact person: "Hei,", not "Til rette vedkommende" (too formal for most contexts)
- Government/academic positions: "Hei," is still fine. "Kjære [tittel] [Etternavn]," if the ad is very formal

## 3. Opening (Innledning)

The opening must hook within the first sentence. Norwegian recruiters spend 6-20 seconds on initial scan, the opening decides if they read on.

### Strong opening patterns

**Achievement hook (strongest for candidates with results):**
"Som [rolle/student] hos [sted] har jeg [konkret resultat]. Nå søker jeg [stilling] hos [bedrift] fordi [spesifikk grunn]."

**Company knowledge hook (strongest when you know the company):**
"Da jeg så at [bedrift] søker [stilling], ble jeg rett og slett glad, [spesifikk grunn knyttet til bedriften]."

**Personal connection hook (strongest with a genuine connection):**
"Etter en hyggelig samtale med [kontaktperson] ble jeg enda mer motivert til å søke på [stilling]."

**Pro tip: ring kontaktpersonen.** NAV, NTNU, and Tekna all recommend calling the contact person listed in the ad before writing. This is a distinctly Norwegian practice that shows initiative, gives you insider information, and provides a natural opening line. Suggest this to the user if a contact person is listed. But Tekna warns: call with good, specific questions, not just for the sake of calling.

**Curiosity hook (good for internships):**
"[Spesifikt aspekt ved stillingen] treffer midt i det jeg brenner for å lære mer om, og som studieløpet mitt innen [fagfelt] har forberedt meg på."

### Opening sentences to NEVER use

These are identified by Norwegian recruiters as instant credibility-killers:

- "Viser til utlyst stilling på Finn.no" ❌
- "Jeg søker med dette på stillingen som..." ❌
- "Jeg har med stor interesse lest stillingsannonsen deres" ❌
- "Jeg skriver for å søke på stillingen..." ❌
- "Stillingen fanget umiddelbart min interesse" ❌ (flagged as AI pattern)
- "Med dette ønsker jeg å søke på..." ❌

## 4. Middle (Hoveddel)

### Address qualifications in priority order
1. First: Every nødvendig kvalifikasjon (must-have) from the ad
2. Then: Relevant ønskede kvalifikasjoner (nice-to-haves)
3. Finally: Bonus strengths not in the ad but clearly relevant

### Use STAR method for examples
Each example should follow this pattern in 2-3 sentences max:

**S**ituasjon: Set the scene briefly
**O**ppgave (T): What was your task/challenge  
**H**andling (A): What you specifically did
**R**esultat: What happened, with numbers if possible

**Example for an internship applicant:**
"I et prosjekt på studiet bygde jeg en maskinlæringsmodell for å klassifisere risiko i boligmarkedet ved hjelp av data fra SSB. Modellen oppnådde 87% nøyaktighet, og prosjektet ga meg praktisk erfaring med Python, scikit-learn og datavisualisering."

Note: Avoid course codes (like IN3050, MAT1080) in the søknad itself. Recruiters outside academia do not know what they mean. Describe the project and its results instead. Course codes belong in the CV.

### Mirror ad keywords
If the ad says "samarbeidsevner" → write "samarbeidsevner"
If the ad says "analytisk" → write "analytisk"  
If the ad says "skytjenester" → write "skytjenester"
Never substitute synonyms. Use their exact words woven naturally into your sentences.

### For internship candidates specifically
- University projects are legitimate experience. Present them with the same seriousness as work experience.
- Part-time jobs show work ethic, connect transferable skills to the role (leadership from teaching, customer focus from retail, reliability from any job)
- Volunteer work counts. Open source contributions count. Hackathons count.
- Emphasize learning speed and curiosity over depth of experience, this is what employers expect from interns

## 5. Closing (Avslutning)

Keep it to 2-3 sentences. Be forward-looking and confident without being demanding.

### Good closing patterns

The best closings are specific to the company, not interchangeable templates. Adapt these:

"Hos [bedrift] vil [type arbeid] handle om [noe konkret og spesifikt for bedriften]. Det appellerer til meg. Jeg stiller gjerne til en samtale.

Med vennlig hilsen
[Fullt navn]"

"Jeg tror bakgrunnen min innen [felt] og erfaringen med [relevant ting] passer godt til det dere beskriver. Jeg ser frem til muligheten for en samtale.

Med vennlig hilsen
[Fullt navn]"

The key: reference something specific about the company or role in the closing, not just your own qualifications. A closing that could be sent to 50 companies is too generic.

### Closing anti-patterns
- "Jeg håper dere vurderer søknaden min" ❌ (too meek)
- "Jeg er den perfekte kandidaten for denne stillingen" ❌ (too strong, violates Janteloven)
- "Kontakt meg gjerne for mer informasjon" ❌ (passive, you should be the one wanting the meeting)
- "Har du spørsmål er det bare å ta kontakt" ❌ (positions you as serving them, not as a peer)

### Sign-off
Always: "Med vennlig hilsen", the safest, most universally appropriate Norwegian sign-off.
Acceptable alternatives: "Vennlig hilsen", "Med beste hilsener"
Never: "Hilsen" alone (too casual), "Med respekt" (too formal), "Mvh" (too abbreviated for a søknad)

## 6. Janteloven Filter

Before finalizing, run every claim through this filter. Norwegian culture penalizes self-promotion more harshly than most cultures. The research shows employers are more skeptical of candidates who present an idealized self-image.

### The rule: Replace claims with evidence

| ❌ Claim (too bold) | ✅ Evidence (Janteloven-safe) |
|---------------------|-------------------------------|
| "Jeg er en dyktig programmerer" | "Gjennom fire semestre med programmeringsemner har jeg bygget tre fullstendige prosjekter i Python og Java" |
| "Jeg er en fantastisk leder" | "Som hovedinstruktør koordinerte jeg 11 instruktører og vi forbedret gjennomføringen av kursene" |
| "Jeg har sterke analytiske evner" | "I statistikkemnet analyserte jeg datasett med R og fikk A" |
| "Jeg er veldig motivert" | "Jeg søkte meg til dette feltet etter å ha brukt fritiden på et sideprosjekt innen [tema]" |

### Collaborative language
Prefer "vi" and "bidra" framing:
- "bidra til teamets mål" over "oppnå resultater"
- "samarbeide om" over "ta ansvar for"
- "vi fikk til" over "jeg oppnådde"

But do not erase your individual contribution entirely, the recruiter needs to know what YOU did. Balance: "I teamprosjektet hadde jeg ansvar for [din del], og sammen leverte vi [resultat]."

## 7. Cliché Blacklist

Block these phrases. If you catch yourself writing any of them, rewrite the sentence.

### Personality clichés (say nothing without examples)
- "Jeg er selvstendig" ❌
- "Jeg jobber like godt alene som sammen med andre" ❌
- "Jeg er løsningsorientert" ❌
- "Jeg er en lagspiller" ❌
- "Jeg brenner for..." ❌
- "Jeg er strukturert og analytisk" ❌
- "Jeg er en sosial person" ❌
- "Jeg er resultatorientert" ❌
- "Jeg er proaktiv" ❌

If you need to express any of these qualities, do it through a concrete example instead.

### Filler words to cut
Remove these entirely, they weaken the text:
- "blant annet"
- "egentlig"  
- "osv."
- "bare"
- "kanskje"
- "eventuelt"
- "for så vidt"

### Corporate buzzwords to avoid
- "synergi"
- "paradigmeskifte"
- "leveranser"
- "stakeholdere" (use "interessenter" if needed)
- "optimalisere" (use "forbedre")
- "implementere" (use "innføre" or "ta i bruk")

## 8. Anti-AI Detection Rules

Norwegian recruiters say 86% can detect AI-written applications. These rules make the output pass as human-written.

### Sentence structure variation
- Mix short and long sentences. AI tends toward uniform medium-length.
- Use an occasional one-sentence paragraph for emphasis.
- Start sentences with different words, AI overuses "Jeg", "I", "Med", "Som"

### Details only the applicant would know
Every søknad MUST include at least 2-3 details that could not come from the job ad alone:
- A specific project name, course code, or tool version
- A concrete number from their experience
- A personal anecdote or observation about the company
- A named person they spoke to or worked with

### Avoid AI-telltale patterns
- **NEVER use em dashes or en dashes (the long horizontal lines often used as separators in text).** This is the single most obvious marker of AI-generated text. Use commas, periods, colons, or parentheses instead. No exceptions.
- No balanced contrasts: "both X and Y", "not only X but also Y" ❌
- No strings of intensifiers: "svært motivert, svært engasjert og svært strukturert" ❌
- No formulaic transitions: "Videre vil jeg trekke frem...", "I tillegg til dette..." ❌
- No perfectly parallel sentence structures across paragraphs
- No English-influenced phrasing: "Jeg er passionert for" ❌ (use "Jeg interesserer meg for")
- Avoid the word "videre" as a paragraph connector

### Rhythm test
Read the søknad aloud. If every sentence has the same rhythm and length, rewrite. Natural Norwegian writing has varied cadence, some sentences punchy, others flowing.

## 9. Formatting

When creating a PDF or docx file, apply these rules:

- **Font:** Helvetica (preferred), Arial, Calibri, Georgia, or Times New Roman. Use one font throughout.
- **Font size:** 10 to 12pt for body text
- **Margins:** 1 inch (2.54cm) on all sides. If struggling to fit on one page, reduce top and bottom to 3/4 inch (1.9cm), but never less.
- **Alignment:** Left-justified (venstrejustert), never centered or justified
- **Spacing:** Clear spacing between the header/address block, each paragraph, and the sign-off
- **Length:** Maximum one A4 page, no exceptions
- **Filename:** FornavnEtternavn_Søknad_Stillingstittel.pdf (or .docx)
- **Language:** Match the ad exactly: bokmål, nynorsk, or English
- **No:** colors, graphics, logos, headers/footers, design elements, or multiple fonts

If submitting to a digital portal (Teamtailor, Jobylon, Webcruiter), warn the user to check how formatting renders after pasting. Portals often strip formatting.

## 10. Final Checklist

Before presenting the draft, verify:

- [ ] 300-350 words, 3-4 paragraphs (up to 400 if the ad specifies content requirements)
- [ ] Clear "rød tråd", a coherent narrative from background to this role
- [ ] Opens with a concrete hook, not a cliché
- [ ] Every must-have qualification from the ad is addressed
- [ ] At least one concrete example with a measurable result
- [ ] Keywords from the ad are mirrored (exact words)
- [ ] Company-specific detail is included (not generic flattery)
- [ ] No sentence starts with "Jeg" more than twice in a row
- [ ] No items from the cliché blacklist
- [ ] No AI-telltale patterns (balanced contrasts, intensifier strings)
- [ ] Zero em dashes or en dashes anywhere in the text
- [ ] Janteloven filter applied, claims backed by evidence
- [ ] Passes the blush test: slightly bold, fully defensible
- [ ] Sign-off is "Med vennlig hilsen" + full name
- [ ] Language matches the ad (bokmål/nynorsk/English)
- [ ] At least 2 details only this applicant would know
- [ ] No course codes (IN3050, MAT1080 etc.), describe projects by content, not by course
- [ ] Read-aloud rhythm test: varied sentence length and structure
- [ ] Zero spelling errors, Norwegian recruiters rank this as the #2 rejection trigger after generic content. Double-check all Norwegian characters (æ, ø, å) and common mistakes (da/når, og/å, de/dem)
